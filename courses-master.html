Cyber-physical Systems Security

SCS600
Completed
7.5 ECTS

Overview

Focuses on main cybersecurity aspects of IoT, Industrial Internet, Smart Cities, Smart Grid, and connected systems. Students learn security architecture principles, network security, and cyber-physical security concepts including physical, logical, and organizational security.

Learning Outcomes

  • Describe various types of cyber-physical systems and their applications
  • Identify vulnerabilities and threats in cyber-physical systems
  • Differentiate between physical, logical, and organizational security
  • Select proper tools and methods to counteract threats
  • Understand impact of cyber-physical systems on society

Assessment

Individual written exam, laboratory work in groups with presentations, and group seminars with oral presentations.

Principles of Cybersecurity

PFC610
Completed
7.5 ECTS

Overview

Provides a broad overview of cybersecurity and its relation to IT security. Covers threats, systems and their weaknesses, preventive defenses (cryptography, AAA, firewalls), and organizational defenses. Based on Cisco Network Academy's CyberOps Associate program.

Learning Outcomes

  • Describe cybersecurity concepts, terminology, and threat phases
  • Explain common threats to networks and end devices
  • Classify different types of cyber security attacks
  • Analyze data communication intrusions and identify vulnerabilities
  • Use monitoring tools to identify different types of attacks

Assessment

Individual written exam, laboratory work in groups with presentations, and group seminars with oral presentations.

Cloud Security

SIM600
In Progress
7.5 ECTS

Overview

Covers cybersecurity in cloud services and systems with hands-on experience in live cloud environments. Includes introduction to research methodology applied to academic study of cloud security topics.

Learning Outcomes

  • Explain cloud computing architectures and security principles
  • Describe cybersecurity threats and vulnerabilities in cloud systems
  • Setup and configure identity and authentication services
  • Identify, manage, and protect cloud data and assets
  • Use methods and tools to identify and reduce vulnerabilities

Assessment

Individual written exam, lab assignments in groups with oral examination, and project with individual oral and written examination.

Privacy, Law, Policy & Compliance

SLP600
In Progress
7.5 ECTS

Overview

Provides overview of laws and ethical issues related to cybersecurity. Identifies policies, rules, and procedures that ensure connected devices and networks meet acceptable security levels based on emerging cybersecurity frameworks.

Learning Outcomes

  • Recognize and explain major legal aspects of data privacy
  • Identify and interpret cybersecurity policy concepts
  • Understand differences between standards and regulations
  • Suggest effective mechanisms to respond to cybersecurity risks
  • Critically reflect on social and ethical aspects of privacy

Assessment

Individual written exam and project with written report and oral presentation in group.
