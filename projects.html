  • 4.0: Weed detection system

    Due to a Non-disclosure Agreement, I am unable to provide more images of the application. However, I have created a visualization to help you understand my part of the project. The client wanted to receive a heatmap showing where certain weeds are present in farm fields. The fields with too many of these weeds are not allowed to be used by the farmer. The process was as follows: we received drone images from the farm fields in (GEO)TIF format, and from the (GEO)TIF images, we could get the coordinates of the top-left corner of where the image was taken. The images were then converted to PNG format, and my AI model (YOLOv8) performed detection on the PNG images, drawing boxes around the weeds and saving the pixel coordinates of the top-left and bottom-right corners of the detections. I then calculated the center of the detections and converted the pixel coordinates to geographical coordinates. Finally, I created a GeoJSON file that could be used by my teammates to create the heatmap.

  • Big Data: Image Classifier

    For this project, we trained an image classifier that predicts between big cats (jaguars, lions, snow leopards, and tigers). To train our AI model, we first built a web scraper, which we used to scrape images from Google to build a dataset. We then used this dataset to build our AI model, and used Streamlit to turn it into a web app. The web application allows you to upload an image of either of these big cats, and it will output a predicted class and the probability. We trained this model to try and beat Google's "Teachable Machine," in which we succeeded.
